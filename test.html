1) Problem Statement

Goal: Identify which early-game (10-minute) features most strongly influence match outcome (blueWins) in high-ELO ranked games.

Why it matters: actionable priorities for players/analysts (objective control, economy, vision) and interpretable benchmarks for esports analytics.

2) Dataset & Features

Source: Kaggle — League of Legends Diamond Ranked Games (10-min). Each row is one match snapshot at 10:00.

  • Target: blueWins (1=blue victory, 0=red victory). Optional mirror redWins is perfectly redundant (redWins=1-blueWins).
  • Raw features: side-specific metrics (e.g., blueGold, redGold, blueDragons, redDragons, …).
  • Engineered: symmetric difference features: diffX = blueX − redX (e.g., diffTotalGold, diffDragons, diffWardsPlaced, …).

Tip: difference features reduce redundancy and make signs intuitive (positive favors blue; negative favors red).

4) Modeling Approach

Logistic Regression (scaled)

Linear and interpretable. Standardize numeric features so coefficients represent effects per 1 standard deviation. Convert coefficients via exp(coef) to get odds ratios.

  • Pros: easy to explain & test (p-values)
  • Cons: can't capture nonlinear thresholds or interactions

Random Forest & SHAP

Tree ensemble for nonlinear effects. Use SHAP (Shapley values) to interpret how each feature pushes predictions up or down.

  • Pros: captures interactions & thresholds
  • Cons: raw importances need SHAP for directionality

5) Logistic Regression — How to Read the Results

Important steps and interpretation tips:

  1. Standardize numeric features: (x - mean)/SD. Coefficients are per 1 SD.
  2. Convert coefficient to odds ratio: odds = exp(coef).
  3. Percent change in odds: (odds - 1) * 100%.
  4. Report practical unit conversions for raw features (e.g., per 1000 gold).

Example (from your model)

Given diffDragons coef = 0.2403:

  • Odds ratio = exp(0.2403) ≈ 1.272
  • Interpretation: each extra dragon advantage at 10 minutes multiplies the odds of a Blue win by ≈ 1.27 (≈ +27% odds).
# Example: convert coef to % change (Python)

pct = (odds - 1) * 100
print(odds, pct)  # 1.272, 27.2%

6) Random Forest & SHAP — Practical Interpretation

Random Forest feature importance ranks predictive power but doesn't give effect direction. Use SHAP to see whether high values of a feature increase or decrease predicted win probability.

SHAP Summary Plot

How to read it:

  • Features sorted by mean |SHAP| (importance).
  • Each dot is one match; color = feature value (red=high, blue=low).
  • Dots to the right increase predicted probability of Blue win; left decrease it.

7) Comparing Methods — Why they may disagree

Short summary:

  • Logistic Regression finds linear, additive signals and reports statistical significance.
  • Random Forest finds split-based importance (may favor features with many distinct values).
  • SHAP reconciles magnitude + direction for the tree model; use it to explain RF output.

Recommendation: trust features that are consistently ranked high across multiple methods. For features ranked differently, inspect SHAP dependence plots to understand threshold effects.

8) Key Results & Practical Takeaways

  1. Top predictors: generally diffTotalGold, diffDragons, and diffExperience.
  2. Actions: prioritize early objective control (dragons) and cry to the CS gods for economy—these correlate strongly with win probability.
  3. Vision: wards at 10 minutes are weak predictors alone; combine with later metrics for full picture.

9) Limitations & Next Steps

  • 10-minute snapshot: does not model momentum or comeback dynamics.
  • No champion or role normalization: some champions naturally have higher gold/CS at 10:00.
  • Patch/meta differences: results may change across patches or regions.

Next steps

  • Expand to minute-by-minute series and use time-series models.
  • Role/champion features; normalize by expected values for role.
  • Calibrate predicted probabilities and add decision thresholds for actionable alerts (e.g., surrender recommendation when win probability < 10%).
